# Orys Learning Architecture™ — 6-Layer Methodology Specification

*Arquitetura de Aprendizagem Orys™*

The methodological backbone of the Orys™ engine, developed by Dr. de Souza (2025).

**Orys Learning Architecture™** is the integration layer that orchestrates Bloom's Taxonomy, the 4 knowledge dimensions (Anderson & Krathwohl), the 10 Orys Patterns™, the Big Five soft skills, and Learning Analytics indicators into a single coherent class architecture for the AI-mediated classroom.

---

## The 3 integrative movements

1. **Compreender** *(Understand)* — recognize behaviors interfering with learning
2. **Interpretar** *(Interpret)* — apply theoretical lenses to those behaviors
3. **Organizar** *(Organize)* — design experiences that modulate behaviors while developing competencies

---

## The 6 Architectural Layers

### 🔸 Architectural Layer 1 — Activation
*Camada Arquitetural 1 — Ativação*

**Default duration:** ~10–15% of total class time

**Purpose:** Establish a trigger that connects pre-class scaffolding to in-class cognition.

**Standard structure:**
- One **trigger question** that names the cognitive operation expected
- A **rapid pair-share dynamic** (3–4 quick items students identify from pre-class work)
- An **initial evidence formula** — a sentence template the students complete:
  > *"When I observe ___, this may relate to ___ because ___."*

**Theoretical grounding:** Ausubel (1968) — advance organizers; Bergmann & Sams (2012) — flipped classroom activation.

---

### 🔸 Architectural Layer 2 — Bloom Strategies for the Verb
*Camada Arquitetural 2 — Estratégias Indicadas para o Verbo de Bloom*

**Default duration:** ~25–30% of total class time

**Purpose:** Deploy active-learning strategies matched to the Bloom verb chosen.

**Standard structure:**
- **4–6 activity types** drawn from the active-learning canon (Visual Thinking, Big Idea, Storytelling, Applied Hard Skills, Structural Representation, Case Analysis, Simulation, Argumentation Rounds, etc.) — chosen to match the verb's cognitive demands.
- **Expected product** — a defined deliverable with 4–5 components
- **Official verb criteria** — typical assessment dimensions for that Bloom verb (e.g., for ANALYZE: clarity of connections, systemic coherence, conceptual grounding, logical traceability)

**Theoretical grounding:** Anderson & Krathwohl (2001); Bloom (1956); Biggs (2003) — Constructive Alignment.

---

### 🔸 Architectural Layer 3 — Orys Pattern™ Intervention
*Camada Arquitetural 3 — Intervenção do Padrão Orys™*

**Default duration:** ~15–20% of total class time

**Purpose:** Transform the active Orys Pattern™ via a structured intervention.

**Standard structure:**
- **Named active pattern** (Orys-1 through Orys-10)
- **Behavioral objective** of the intervention (what we want to transform)
- **Collaboration structure** — role assignments (rotating, with cognitive responsibilities)
- **Mandatory micro-deliverable** — a sentence formula the group must produce together
- **Neurodidactic strategies mobilized** (collaborative roles, pair/trio synthesis, brief co-authorship, minimal structures)

**Theoretical grounding:** depends on the pattern (see `orys-patterns.md`). Always includes Goleman (1995) — Emotional Intelligence + de Souza (2025) — Orys Pattern definition.

---

### 🔸 Architectural Layer 4 — Initial Quick Evidence Criteria
*Camada Arquitetural 4 — Critérios Iniciais para Evidência Rápida*

**Default duration:** ~10% of total class time

**Purpose:** Establish formative criteria the instructor can observe in real time.

**Standard structure:**
- **5 orienting criteria** (observable, not vague):
  1. Clarity of connections
  2. Systemic coherence
  3. Minimal conceptual grounding
  4. Logical traceability
  5. Real collaborative participation with identifiable contribution

**Theoretical grounding:** Black & Wiliam (1998) — Formative Assessment; Sadler (1989).

---

### 🔸 Architectural Layer 5 — Procedural Training
*Camada Arquitetural 5 — Treino Procedimental*

**Default duration:** ~25–30% of total class time

**Purpose:** Move from conceptual analysis to procedural application.

**Standard structure:**
- **Activity** — hands-on / case-based / simulation / role-play
- **Training script** (5 steps typical):
  1. Observe
  2. Identify (signs / variables)
  3. Relate to mechanism / theory
  4. Simulate / apply
  5. Explain reasoning aloud
- **Product** — brief demonstration + verbal justification (~1 minute per student)

**Theoretical grounding:** Kolb (1984) — Experiential Learning Cycle; Knowles (1980) — Andragogy (problem-centered learning); Schön (1983) — Reflective Practitioner.

---

### 🔸 Architectural Layer 6 — Metacognition
*Camada Arquitetural 6 — Metacognição*

**Default duration:** ~5% of total class time

**Purpose:** Surface awareness of one's own cognitive structure.

**Standard structure:**
- **One metacognitive question** asking the student to identify the hardest connection or gap
- **Metacognitive indicator** — awareness of structural analysis (recognizes gaps, identifies where group was needed)

**Theoretical grounding:** Flavell (1979) — Metacognition; Brookfield (1995) — Critical Reflection; Schön (1983).

## The 4 knowledge dimensions (Anderson & Krathwohl, 2001)

Every Orys Learning Architecture™ class plan integrates all four:

| Domain | Manifestation example (subject-agnostic) |
|---|---|
| **Factual** | Data, signs, indicators, scenario specifics, case findings |
| **Conceptual** | Theory-to-theory relations within the discipline |
| **Procedural** | Observation, analysis, technique, application |
| **Metacognitive** | Awareness of one's own reasoning, gaps, group-mediated improvements |

## The 3-level sufficiency rubric structure

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Sufficient** | Student demonstrates the verb fully, with grounded justification |
| **Partial** | Student demonstrates partial mastery; recoverable gap |
| **Insufficient** | Student fails to operate the verb; intervention required |

Each criterion in the rubric is **observable** (the instructor can SEE it) and aligned to the Bloom verb + the active Orys Pattern™ when relevant.

## Authoritative Bloom × Big Five Mapping (de Souza, 2025)

**This is the canonical 1-to-1 mapping used by Orys Learning Architecture™.** Each Bloom verb activates ONE primary Big Five trait. The mapping is theoretically grounded in Costa & McCrae (1992) and operationalized in de Souza (2025).

| Bloom level | Primary Big Five trait | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Know / Remember** | **Extroversion** | Communication, sharing of recall, talk-aloud of what is known |
| **Understand** | **Extroversion** | Explanation, structured communication of understanding |
| **Apply** | **Conscientiousness** | Focus, responsibility, task completion, procedural follow-through |
| **Analyze** | **Emotional Stability** | Handling complexity, uncertainty, contradictory evidence without abandoning reasoning |
| **Evaluate** | **Agreeableness** | Empathy, respectful disagreement, listening, integrating stakeholder perspectives |
| **Innovate / Create** | **Openness to the New** | Creativity, flexibility, authentic novelty, design of feasible solutions |

### Observable behaviors per trait (embed at the corresponding Architectural Layer)

| Trait | Observable student behavior the instructor can SEE |
|---|---|
| **Extroversion** | Student shares an example/explanation aloud, listens to a peer, adjusts the explanation when asked |
| **Conscientiousness** | Student assumes a role, completes the assigned task, manages time within the group, helps the group reach the goal |
| **Emotional Stability** | Student revises reasoning when finding contradictions or new evidence, does NOT abandon the task when uncertain |
| **Agreeableness** | Student defends a position with evidence AND listens respectfully to opposing views, integrates at least one peer suggestion |
| **Openness to the New** | Student proposes an original, flexible, feasible solution; willing to depart from the obvious answer |

### How to use this in plan output

1. **Pick the Bloom verb** from the user's stated objective
2. **Name the corresponding primary Big Five trait** from the table above (1-to-1)
3. **Embed the trait as an observable behavior** at the Architectural Layer where that Bloom level is activated (Layer 1 if Know/Understand, Layer 2 if Apply/Analyze, Layer 3+5 if Evaluate, Layer 5+6 if Innovate)
4. **Add to the rubric** — one criterion explicitly tagged to the soft skill (e.g., "Demonstrates emotional stability — revises reasoning when contradicted by data")
5. **Add to the Learning Analytics indicators** — one indicator tied to observable trait expression

The plan must NEVER simply list "soft skills developed" at the end — it must EMBED them as observable, assessable behaviors throughout the stages.

## Active Learning Indicators (default checklist)

Every plan should produce a 6-item ☑ checklist confirming:

1. Structural challenge present
2. Active conceptual integration
3. Systemic visualization of relationships
4. Traceable collaborative production
5. Procedural training with justification
6. Conscious reflection on reasoning gaps

## Learning Analytics indicators (standard set)

The plan should produce **10 actionable Learning Analytics indicators**, each with:

- **Indicator name** (what we measure)
- **How to collect** (rubric, checklist, count, observation, self-assessment)
- **Effective-learning signal** (the quantitative or behavioral threshold)

At least **3 indicators** must reference the active Orys Pattern™.

The standard 10 indicators (template — adapt to discipline):

1. Pre-class preparation rate (target: ≥80%)
2. Conceptual connection index
3. Quality of justification
4. Distributed collaborative participation *(Orys-pattern-tagged)*
5. Coherence between case and application
6. Sufficiency evolution (initial → final delivery)
7. Co-authorship density *(Orys-pattern-tagged)*
8. Metacognition indicator
9. Pattern alert *(Orys-pattern-tagged)*
10. Graduate-profile indicator

## ⏱ TIME-BUDGET DISCIPLINE — Strategy Selection by Available Minutes

**CRITICAL PRINCIPLE:** Every active-learning strategy has a minimum viable time. Picking a strategy that doesn't fit the available class time guarantees failure. The Orys engine MUST adjust Architectural Layer 2 + Layer 5 strategy choices based on the user's input [e] (class duration).

### Strategy time-budgets (canonical reference)

| Strategy | Min viable | Sweet spot | Max useful |
|---|---|---|---|
| Think-Pair-Share (TPS) | 3 min | 5–7 min | 10 min |
| Write-Pair-Share | 5 min | 8–12 min | 15 min |
| Group Discussion + notes | 10 min | 15–20 min | 30 min |
| Reading + Discussion Q | 15 min | 25–35 min | 50 min |
| TAR Peer Feedback | 15 min | 25–30 min | 45 min |
| Storytelling / case unpack | 10 min | 20–30 min | 60 min |
| Visual Thinking / concept map | 10 min | 20–30 min | 45 min |
| Simulation / lab procedure | 30 min | 45–60 min | 90 min |
| Jigsaw | 30 min | 45–60 min | 90 min |
| Project-based (PBL) | 1 class | Multiple sessions | Weeks |

### Realistic strategy stacks by total class duration

| Class length | Realistic strategy stack |
|---|---|
| **20 min** | TPS only (5 min) — or pure lecture |
| **30 min** | TPS (5) + 1 quick activity (15) + closure (5) |
| **50 min** | Activation TPS (5) + 1 deeper activity (25) + reflection (5) + transitions |
| **75 min** | TPS (5) + Reading w/ Q (25) + brief peer feedback (20) + synthesis (10) |
| **90 min** | **Full 6 Architectural Layers** — every Layer fits at default % |
| **120 min** | Full sequence + extended Layer 5 (procedural training) |
| **Half-day workshop (3–4h)** | 2 linked Orys Learning Architecture™ sequences |
| **Full-day workshop** | Multiple sequences + integration work |

### Strategy-selection rule for Layer 2 + Layer 5

When generating Architectural Layer 2 (Bloom Strategies) and Architectural Layer 5 (Procedural Training), the engine must:

1. **Compute available minutes** for that Layer (% of total workload from input [e])
2. **Filter the strategy library** to only those whose minimum viable time fits
3. **Select 2–4 strategies** that together fit the available window
4. **Never recommend a strategy whose floor exceeds the available window**

### Strategies COMPETE for time — never over-pack

A class plan that proposes TPS + Group Discussion + Reading + TAR + Simulation in 50 minutes IS BROKEN. The plugin should refuse to generate impossible time budgets and instead warn the user:

> ⚠️ The strategies requested exceed the available time for this Layer. Please pick fewer strategies or extend the workload.
