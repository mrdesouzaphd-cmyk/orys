# The 10 Orys Patterns™ — Reference Catalog

Canonical reference for the 10 Orys Patterns™ — an original academic taxonomy of contemporary classroom behaviors in the AI-mediated, digitally-saturated higher-education environment.

**Source:** de Souza (2025). *Orys Learning Architecture™ Vol. 1.*

**Theoretical lenses underlying the framework:**
- Meaningful Learning — Ausubel
- Emotional Intelligence — Goleman
- Growth Mindset — Dweck
- Core Skills for the Future of Work — OECD / UNESCO / WEF

---

## Orys-1 — Mediated Habitat Anchoring
*(Ancoragem em Habitat Mediado)*

**Observable indicator:** Students prefer screen and asynchronous as the dominant environment, even when in-person activity is viable.

**Theoretical anchors:**
- Turkle (2011) — *Alone Together*
- Gazzaley & Rosen (2016) — *The Distracted Mind*
- Lens: Goleman — Emotional Intelligence

**Bloom verbs that activate it most:** Discuss / Debate / Critique (in-person communication-heavy verbs)

**Intervention (Orys Layer 3):** Structured in-person micro-tasks with mandatory face-to-face deliverables; pre-class digital scaffolding to lower in-person friction.

**Learning Analytics detection:** Ratio of asynchronous-to-synchronous engagement; participation distribution in face-to-face vs. digital channels.

---

## Orys-2 — Dialogic Endurance Threshold
*(Limiar de Resistência Dialógica)*

**Observable indicator:** Difficulty sustaining reasoning in dialogue with divergence and active listening, even without strong signs of emotional threat.

**Theoretical anchors:**
- Mercer & Howe (2012) — Dialogic Teaching
- Alexander (2008) — *Towards Dialogic Teaching*
- Lens: Ausubel — Meaningful Learning (argument as anchor)

**Bloom verbs that activate it most:** Argue / Defend / Evaluate / Justify

**Intervention (Orys Layer 3):** Scaffolded argumentation rounds with role rotation (proposer → challenger → synthesizer); time-boxed structured debate.

**Learning Analytics detection:** Argument-exchange count per group; ratio of student-to-student vs. student-to-instructor turns.

---

## Orys-3 — Exposure Sensitivity Pattern
*(Padrão de Sensibilidade à Exposição)*

**Observable indicator:** Students avoid public exposure even when content is simple.

**Theoretical anchors:**
- Heimberg et al. (2014) — Social Anxiety Research
- Haidt (2024) — *The Anxious Generation*
- Lens: Goleman — Emotional Intelligence

**Bloom verbs that activate it most:** Present / Demonstrate / Defend

**Intervention (Orys Layer 3):** Low-stakes warm-up presentations in pairs before whole-class; written-then-spoken progression; depersonalized voice options (cards, anonymous boards).

**Learning Analytics detection:** Voluntary speaking rate; ratio of called-on to volunteered turns.

---

## Orys-4 — Productive Friction Capacity
*(Capacidade de Fricção Produtiva)*

**Observable indicator:** Students reject error, criticism, uncertainty, and productive discomfort.

**Theoretical anchors:**
- Dweck (2006) — Growth Mindset
- Duckworth (2016) — *Grit*
- Hiebert & Wearne (1993) — Productive Struggle
- Lens: Dweck — Growth Mindset

**Bloom verbs that activate it most:** Analyze / Evaluate / Create (verbs requiring iteration)

**Intervention (Orys Layer 3):** Iterative drafts with explicit "first version is supposed to fail" framing; error-as-data debrief; productive struggle scaffolding with calibrated difficulty.

**Learning Analytics detection:** Iteration count per student; quit-rate at first error; recovery-time after corrective feedback.

---

## Orys-5 — Validation Latency Sensitivity
*(Sensibilidade à Latência de Validação)*

**Observable indicator:** Urgent need for feedback, with affective and cognitive disorganization when feedback is slow or non-continuous.

**Theoretical anchors:**
- Deci & Ryan (2000) — Self-Determination Theory
- Crocker & Park (2004) — Contingent Self-Worth
- Lens: Goleman — Emotional Intelligence

**Bloom verbs that activate it most:** Create / Produce / Compose (verbs with longer output cycles)

**Intervention (Orys Layer 3):** Micro-feedback architecture (peer checkpoints every 5–10 min); rotating "Conceptual Connector" role distributes validation; self-assessment scaffolds before instructor review.

**Learning Analytics detection:** Help-seeking frequency; behavioral markers during feedback delay (off-task, anxious queries); engagement drop-off rate.

---

## Orys-6 — Comparative Reference Distortion
*(Distorção da Referência Comparativa)*

**Observable indicator:** Distorted self-image leading to inhibition, avoidance, and demotivation — even when feedback is available.

**Theoretical anchors:**
- Festinger (1954) — Social Comparison Theory
- Twenge (2017) — *iGen*
- Lens: Dweck — Growth Mindset

**Bloom verbs that activate it most:** Create / Present / Demonstrate (visible, comparable outputs)

**Intervention (Orys Layer 3):** Anonymous peer-work display; criteria-referenced feedback (not normative comparison); individual progress tracking against own baseline.

**Learning Analytics detection:** Self-assessment vs. instructor-assessment delta; voluntary participation drop after peer-output exposure.

---

## Orys-7 — Networked Solitude
*(Solidão em Rede)*

**Observable indicator:** Students manifest a state of loneliness and emotional disconnection even with high connectivity.

**Theoretical anchors:**
- Turkle (2011) — *Alone Together*
- Cacioppo & Patrick (2008) — Loneliness Research
- Lens: Goleman — Emotional Intelligence

**Bloom verbs that activate it most:** Collaborate / Co-create / Co-author

**Intervention (Orys Layer 3):** Stable small-group affiliations across sessions (not random pairing each class); explicit relational rituals (opening check-ins, named contributions); meaningful interdependence in deliverables.

**Learning Analytics detection:** Cross-session group continuity; expressed-affect markers in reflection responses.

---

## Orys-8 — Cognitive Reciprocity Ceiling
*(Teto de Reciprocidade Cognitiva)*

**Observable indicator:** Interactions lacking deep reciprocity — low vulnerability, low trust, low continuity.

**Theoretical anchors:**
- Brown (2012) — Vulnerability Research
- Granovetter (1973) — Strength of Weak Ties
- Lens: Goleman — Emotional Intelligence

**Bloom verbs that activate it most:** Analyze / Synthesize / Co-construct

**Intervention (Orys Layer 3):**
- **Behavioral objective:** Transform surface interaction into collaboration with shared cognitive responsibility.
- **Collaboration structure (rotating trio roles):**
  - **Clinical Reader / Domain Reader** — retrieves data and references
  - **Conceptual Connector** — relates the domain concepts
  - **Procedural Validator** — verifies coherence with action/practice
- **Mandatory micro-deliverable formula:**
  > *"Our group identified that finding ___ relates to ___ because ___; this can be observed or applied through ___."*
- **Neurodidactic strategies:** Collaborative roles, pair/trio synthesis, brief co-authorship, minimal structures.

**Learning Analytics detection:**
- Distributed collaborative participation (all members assume a cognitive function)
- Co-authorship density (contributions traceable by role)
- Shallow-collaboration alert (dominant voice, silent members, mechanical task division)

---

## Orys-9 — Solitary Cognition Pattern
*(Padrão de Cognição Solitária)*

**Observable indicator:** Students organize learning as a solitary, on-demand process, reducing social mediation and collective synthesis — even when they can focus.

**Theoretical anchors:**
- Vygotsky (1978) — Zone of Proximal Development
- Hutchins (1995) — Distributed Cognition
- Lens: Ausubel — Meaningful Learning (meaning needs intersubjectivity)

**Bloom verbs that activate it most:** Synthesize / Integrate / Co-construct

**Intervention (Orys Layer 3):** Mandatory collective synthesis stages (no individual submission until peer review); jigsaw structures where each student holds a piece others need.

**Learning Analytics detection:** Solo-vs-collaborative time ratio; quality difference between solo draft and group-mediated revision.

---

## Orys-10 — Cognitive Idle Aversion
*(Aversão à Ociosidade Cognitiva)*

**Observable indicator:** Aversion to silence, waiting, and dense reading; constant stimulus-seeking.

**Theoretical anchors:**
- Gazzaley & Rosen (2016) — *The Distracted Mind*
- Newport (2016) — *Deep Work*
- Lens: Goleman — Emotional Intelligence (attention as emotional regulation)

**Bloom verbs that activate it most:** Reflect / Analyze (verbs requiring sustained processing)

**Intervention (Orys Layer 3):** Protected silent processing windows with explicit timing; "thinking before responding" scaffolds (write-pair-share); explicit normalization of pause as cognitive work.

**Learning Analytics detection:** Time-to-first-response after question posed; multitasking markers during reflective tasks.
